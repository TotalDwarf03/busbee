Pollution Viewer

Welcome to the Pollution Viewer! This map contains a range of WMS overlays that display pollution data due to Road Transport.

Available Pollution Types

Use the layer toggle in the top right corner of the map to select different pollution types. Available gasses include:

  • Carbon Dioxide (CO2)
  • Nitrogen Oxides (NOx)
  • Particulate Matter (PM2.5)
  • Particulate Matter (PM10)
  • Methane (CH4)
  • Nitrous Oxide (N2O)

Viewing Recommendations

While viewing overlays, I recommend only selecting one layer at a time to avoid cluttering the map.

What the Data Shows

The pollution data displayed on the map provides insights into the levels of various pollutants in different areas. By analyzing this data, we can better understand the impact of road transport on air quality and identify areas that may require further investigation or intervention.

Generally, across pollution types, higher levels of pollution are observed in urban areas with dense traffic. This includes areas such as London, Birmingham, Glasgow and (you guessed it) Manchester.

This shows that road transport is a significant contributor to air pollution in cities, leading to increased health risks for residents. Efforts to reduce vehicle emissions, such as promoting public transport and encouraging the use of electric vehicles, could help improve air quality in these areas.

Linking back to this site's main focus on bus travel, buses can play a crucial role in reducing road transport pollution. By providing efficient and accessible public transportation options, buses can help decrease the number of private vehicles on the road, leading to lower emissions and improved air quality.
